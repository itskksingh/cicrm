specializes in: 

Joint Replacement (Arthroplasty): Focusing on "resurfacing" joints worn out by arthritis. Advanced techniques now allow for uninterrupted muscle-sparing surgery, where the surgeon works between muscles rather than cutting through them.

Spine Surgery: Shifting from large "open" back surgeries to Endoscopic Spine Surgery, where a surgeon can remove a herniated disc through a tube the size of a pencil, often as a walk-in/walk-out procedure.

Fracture Management: Setting broken bones using casts, splints, or internal fixation (plates and screws).
Arthroscopy: Using a tiny camera to diagnose and treat joint issues like torn cartilage or loose bone fragments through small incisions.  

Sports Medicine: Repairing ACL tears, meniscus injuries, and rotator cuff repairs.  

Deformity Correction: Treating conditions like clubfoot or limb length discrepancies.  

Pain Management: Joint injections (cortisone, hyaluronic acid), physical therapy coordination, and bracing.

Regenerative Medicine: Using Biological treatments like Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) or Stem Cell therapy to heal tissues without traditional surgery.
